# Setting up your health assistant

This guide gets you from nothing to a working, private health assistant you can talk to. It's
written for someone who is **not** technical. There's no coding, no typing commands. If you can
install an app and drag a file into a folder, you can do this.

Take it one step at a time. The whole thing takes about 20 minutes, and you only do it once.

---

## What you're setting up

A private assistant that lives on **your own computer** and helps you:

- keep all your medical records organized in one place,
- write down symptoms or episodes properly, the way a specialist needs them,
- create a clean summary to hand your doctor,
- and (optional) make sense of a 23andMe or AncestryDNA download.

**Your medical information stays in a folder on your computer.** It does not get posted online.

---

## Step 1 — Install the Claude app

1. Go to **claude.ai/download** and install the **Claude** app for your computer (Mac or Windows).
2. Open it and sign in. If you don't have an account, create one.
3. You'll need the **Pro plan** (or higher). The feature we use, called **Cowork**, is included in
   it. You can subscribe from inside the app.

> **Why Pro?** Talking to the assistant costs a small monthly subscription. Having your own account
> also means your health information is tied to *you*, not to whoever set this up for you.

---

## Step 2 — Turn on Cowork

**Cowork** is the mode where Claude can work with files in a folder you choose — that's what makes
this a health archive and not just a chat.

1. In the Claude app, look for **Cowork** (a button or an icon in the sidebar). Click it.
2. If it asks, let it finish getting ready. The first time can take a minute.

---

## Step 3 — Add the health assistant

This is the one slightly technical-looking step. It's just typing a name into a box. Follow it
exactly and it works.

1. In the app, open **Customize** (in the sidebar), then choose **Plugins**.
2. Click **Add marketplace**.
3. In the box, type: **`csells/health-tracker`**  ← *(the person who gave you this will confirm the
   exact name)*
4. It'll show a plugin called **health-archive**. Click **Install**.

That's it — your assistant now knows how to be a health archive.

---

## Step 4 — Make a folder for your health information

1. On your computer, create a new folder somewhere you'll remember — for example, in Documents,
   called **My Health**.
2. Leave it empty for now. The assistant fills it in for you in the next step.

> Keep this folder on your own computer (or your personal cloud drive). This is where everything
> lives, so put it somewhere it'll be safely backed up — the same place your other important
> documents live is perfect.

---

## Step 5 — Connect the folder and say hello

1. In Cowork, connect the **My Health** folder you just made. (There's a "work in a folder" or
   folder button — pick your folder.)
2. Now just type this to Claude:

   > **Set up my health archive.**

3. It will ask you some friendly questions — your name, the main thing going on with your health,
   any medications and allergies, and whether anything in your medical records is wrong or missing.
   Answer what you can; **"I don't know" or "skip that" is always a fine answer.**

When it's done, your folder will be neatly organized and ready to use.

---

## That's it. Here's what you can do now

Just talk to Claude in plain language whenever you need to:

- **Add records:** put PDFs (lab results, visit notes, anything) into the **intake** folder inside
  your health folder, then say *"file my new records."*
- **Write down a symptom:** *"I had an episode last night, help me log it."*
- **Get a doctor summary:** *"Make me a health report for my appointment."*
- **Understand your DNA (optional):** download your **raw data** file from 23andMe or AncestryDNA,
  put it in the **records → genome** folder, and say *"analyze my DNA."*

---

## A few things worth knowing

- **Your records are safe.** The assistant is built to *never* delete or change the original
  documents you add. It only ever adds to them.
- **Keep your data private.** In the Claude app's settings, you can turn off using your
  conversations to improve their AI — worth doing, given what's in here. (Look under Privacy.)
- **This isn't a doctor.** Everything it makes is meant to help you have better conversations with
  your real doctors — not to replace them. Anything that looks serious should go to a professional.
- **If something breaks or looks wrong,** you can always ask Claude in plain words ("this doesn't
  look right, can you help?"). You won't break anything by asking.

---

## If you get stuck on Step 3

Adding the marketplace is the only fiddly part. If the name doesn't work or you don't see the
plugin, double-check you typed it exactly, then click **Update** on the marketplace. If it still
won't appear, ask the person who shared this with you to confirm the exact marketplace name — that's
the one piece they need to get right for you.
